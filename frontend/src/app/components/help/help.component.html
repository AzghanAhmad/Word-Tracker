üñãÔ∏è Creating Your Perfect Plan

The foundation of AuthorFLOW is the Writing Plan. When you create a plan, you're not just setting a goal; you're designing your workflow.

  • Goal Types: Choose between "Word Count" for draft goals or "Time" for research and editing phases.
  • Flexible Days: You can exclude weekends or specific weekdays if you know you won't be writing then.
  • Strategy Recalculation: If you miss a day, AuthorFLOW automatically adjusts your future daily targets to keep you on track without feeling overwhelmed.

üìä Mastery of Strategies

Choosing the right strategy is key to avoiding burnout:

Steady: The classic approach. Your remaining goal is divided equally by remaining days. Best for consistent daily habits.
Rising (Mountain): Starts small (e.g., 200 words) and peaks in the middle of your plan. Great for building momentum.
Random (Chaos): Targets vary by up to 30% each day. Keeps things interesting for authors who hate routine.

  • Monthly Heatmap: A visual grid of your consistency. Focus on keeping the "green streak" alive!

üöÄ Getting Started

Can I have multiple projects active at once?

Yes! AuthorFLOW supports unlimited active projects. You can manage a novel draft, a screenplay, and a blog series all from the same dashboard.

How do I delete or archive a finished plan?

Go to the Plan Detail page. In the header, you'll find "Archive" and "Delete" buttons. Archiving keeps your history but hides the plan from the main dashboard, while Deleting removes it permanently.

What happens if I write more than my daily target?

Congratulations! Your future daily targets will automatically decrease slightly to maintain your original deadline, or you can finish the project ahead of schedule.

Can I sync my word count from Scrivener or MS Word?

Currently, word counts must be entered manually. We found this manual "check-in" helps authors stay more mindful of their progress. Automatic sync features for major writing software are in our long-term roadmap!

How do Group Challenges work?

Challenges are shared goals. When you join a challenge, your daily logs for matching projects automatically contribute to the group total. You can see how you rank against other participants on the leaderboard.
